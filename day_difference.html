The following graphs show the number of days between the target Sentinel-2 tile and the latest (closest in time) clear historical tile from the same place. A target tile is a 512x512px area cropped from the full Sentinel-2 product. Every Sentinel-2 product from July to November was cropped into target tiles. It is not distinguished whether the target tile itself is cloudy or clear. A clear tile is a 512x512px tile from April to November, which does not contain clouds, semitransparent clouds or cloud shadows.

All the Sentinel-2 products from April to November from a given year were downloaded, predicted with kappamask and cropped into tiles. The pipeline and cloud prediction were fully automatic.

The left and right panel of each image contain the same information, but the right one shows the accumulated view.

This parameter is the maximum wind gust at the specified time, at a height of ten metres above the surface of the Earth. The WMO defines a wind gust as the maximum of the wind averaged over 3 second intervals. This duration is shorter than a model time step, and so the ECMWF Integrated Forecasting System (IFS) deduces the magnitude of a gust within each time step from the time-step-averaged surface stress, surface friction, wind shear and stability. Care should be taken when comparing model parameters with observations, because observations are often local to a particular point in space and time, rather than representing averages over a model grid box.
